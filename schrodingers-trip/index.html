  • Leading up to this trip was very interesting. Andre’s family came to Beijing, so I decided to give them most of my clothes. All the clothes I left had to fit in a backpack. I didn’t have to pay for the extra luggage.

  • I had several deadlines that would happen in the middle of the trip. I got rid of the first one as soon as I could what that meant was that I did an all nighter right before the train to Shanghai. I manage to start and finish my Big Data homework in one night. Then, packed my stuff and even went to buy a Tsinghua hoodie as I didn’t have any winter clothes with me anymore. The train would depart early morning to Shanghai and then we had another train to Hangzhou. Funny enough, I didn’t feel the need to sleep in the train, I must’ve slept for 1-2 hours during the day. I worked on the Deep Learning homework most of the time.

  • Hangzhou

    • Day 1:
      • When we got the train to Hangzhou, we had some random conversations with Chinese people in the train. Everyone was interested in what we had to say, not only the people in our table but around our table as well.
      • We got a bus and went straight to fake paris. It’s what the name implies, even Google Photos recognizes the photos I have from there as actually being in Paris.
      • We got a bus back to the West Lake, saw a pagoda and then found out about a show that happened near the west lake. As, we didn’t want to pay, we managed to find a way around the stage, and we could see the show from the side without having to pay.
      • I worked for a bit in a KFC and then we decided to go to sleep. As the west lake is mostly green on the map. We just wanted to find a random place where we could set tent and sleep there. It wasn’t a task as easy as we thought it would be and we wandered about for a good time. I remember going through bamboo forests in the dark. We found a place, we thought was good enough since it seemed to be far away from the road.
      • We slept there, then it started to rain in the middle of the night. We didn’t do anything about it and just tried to sleep, the tent started to get wet. I remember shivering a bit.
    • Day 2:
      • When we finally woke up, we found out that we were in a park and we could hear people walking around. We quickly packed our stuff, ate breakfast underneath a pavilion and left.
      • This day is and was one of my favorites. The day was mostly us carrying around our heavy backpack going in the direction of tea villages. The daylio entry makes justice to it, I don’t have anything else to add. The atmosphere of those misty tea fields and bamboo forests as we mostly walked alone through those paths that connected villages. The temples we went were deep in the middle of the forest and we were covered with moss.
      • When it started to get dark, and we finally decided to settle and camp nearby a tea village. We went to buy noodles from a shop and the owner got us warm water, so we could eat them.
      • We decided to camp in the middle of the tea fields that were in the mountains surrounding the village as the village was in a valley. We followed the path that we found that went behind some houses but then the path disappeared and there was no artificial light on the path to guide us, only the moon and light pollution from the village. We just savagely climbed between each “trench” of tea until we found a place that was plane and big enough for us to fit our tent, as soon as we did we camped there and cracked a cold one. It had been a good and strenuous day indeed.
    • Day 3:
      • After a “good nights rest”, we woke up early and went back to the village to get a bus. I dropped my phone when going down as I was taking a picture.
      • We finished exploring around the west lake itself. Afterwards, we decided to go to a coffee shop to get some work done. I got deep learning homework to work. I have a image of my dusty screen that showed the model converging that I sent to the Andre and Francisco.
      • Afterwards, we got a taxi and went to the train station, and arrived at Shanghai after not taking a shower for three days.

When coming back, I fell asleep in the taxi and the taxi driver woke me up as we had arrived. In a hurry I left the taxi and started walking towards the AirBnB. Where then he shouted at me to come to him but I said “fuck off”. As we were getting back home, I realize I didn’t have my glasses with me and that I had left them in the taxi. I call him back and tried with help of google translate to try and meet up somewhere and somehow it worked and I got my glasses back.

  • Anhui

    • Day 7:
      • Since the train was late we arrived in the middle of night, we got “scammed” by the hostel, the place didn’t exist, so we didn’t have any place to sleep. And to be honest, we had planned to trick the hostel as well, as the reservation was for 3 people who went to find the place but we were going to try to sneak two more in (me and francisco)
      • We weren’t finding a decent place to sleep, so we decided to grab a DiDi to go Hongcun and camp nearby the village. We split where 2 people stayed back trying to find a place to sleep in a hostel and 3 people when to the village.
      • We asked the DiDi driver to let us leave on the side and not inside the village. As he goes around the village and we leave the DiDi, a security guard approaches us in a bike.
      • He asks us for our ticket. We didn’t have one, we didn’t even plan on entering the village at night, since we couldn’t camp in the middle of the village. So, how do we get rid of this guy? We couldn’t say we were camping, since wild camping is technically illegal in China. We decide to buy the ticket, so the guy stops pestering us. We buy the ticket, but don’t enter the village and leave.
      • Obviously, when we leave and don’t enter, the guy gets suspicious. We find a way into the some fields and cross a bridge. We see his flashlight, nearby the of the path we went into and pointing around the field. We see him go back and forth along the main road with motorbike trying to find us. Meanwhile, we go forth along the path without any light, so he couldn’t find us. We find an abandoned house, some sort of cottage.
      • We get paranoid if it’s a good idea or not for us to go inside the house (the house was falling apart) and find a room to set camp. Meanwhile, we get a call from the other two saying that they didn’t find a place and are coming. They are able to somehow sleep in the ticket office.
      • Eventually, after much back and forth, we decided to camp inside.
